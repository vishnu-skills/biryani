Popular Types of Biryani in India

  • Hyderabadi Biryani – Famous for its spicy flavor, made with basmati rice, saffron, and a variety of spices.
  • Lucknowi (Awadhi) Biryani – A subtle and aromatic biryani, cooked in the dum pukht style.
  • Kolkata Biryani – A mild, fragrant biryani with potatoes, slightly sweeter than other versions.
  • Malabar Biryani (Thalassery Biryani) – A coastal biryani from Kerala, known for using small-grain rice (Jeerakasala) and less spice.
  • Ambur Biryani – A Tamil Nadu specialty, slightly tangy with the use of dried red chilies.
  • Dindigul Biryani – A biryani from Tamil Nadu made with a tangy, spicy masala and short-grain rice.
  • Sindhi Biryani – Originating from Sindh (Pakistan), this biryani is known for its spicy and tangy flavor, often made with yogurt and green chilies.
  • Bombay Biryani – A sweeter version of biryani with a mix of fried onions, potatoes, and kewra water.
  • Calicut Biryani – A Kerala-style biryani that uses coconut and is more fragrant than spicy.
  • Arcot Biryani – A Tamil Nadu version made with flavorful spices and short-grain rice.
  • Bhatkali Biryani – From Karnataka, this biryani has layers of rice and meat, and uses a lot of onions and green chilies.
  • Chettinad Biryani – A spicy biryani from Tamil Nadu, made with a mix of freshly ground spices.
  • Kalyani Biryani – A cheaper variant of Hyderabadi biryani, often made with beef or buffalo meat.
  • Memoni Biryani – From Gujarat, this biryani is extremely spicy and made with lamb, yogurt, and potatoes.
  • Mughlai Biryani – Rich and royal, often with nuts, saffron, and dried fruits, a remnant of Mughal cuisine.
  • Bohri Biryani – A community-based dish, often prepared by Bohri Muslims, slightly tangy with yogurt.
  • Palakkad Biryani – A vegetarian biryani from Kerala, made with traditional spices and rice.
  • Rawther Biryani – Popular in Tamil Nadu, known for its strong, robust flavor and made with mutton or chicken.
  • Karnataka Donne Biryani – Served in eco-friendly containers called "donne," with a distinct flavor of green masala and mint.
  • Goan Fish Biryani – A seafood biryani made with local fish, coconut, and spices from Goa.
  • Tahiri Biryani – A vegetarian version, popular in Uttar Pradesh and made with mixed vegetables.
  • Beary Biryani – A Mangalorean biryani from the Beary Muslim community, mildly spiced and often made with chicken.
  • Afghani Biryani – Influenced by Afghan flavors, this biryani is mild and features lamb, raisins, and carrots.
  • Delhi Biryani – A biryani from the capital, spiced delicately with fragrant masalas and sometimes served with boiled eggs.
  • Achi Biryani – A tribal biryani from Tamil Nadu, known for its distinct blend of spices and minimal use of ghee or oil.
  • Kashmiri Biryani – A fragrant biryani from Kashmir, featuring dry fruits, saffron, and mild spices.
